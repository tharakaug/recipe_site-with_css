String Hoppers

String hoppers, also known as "Idiyappam" in Tamil and Malayalam, are a traditional South Indian and Sri Lankan dish. They are a type of steamed rice noodle that is typically served as a breakfast or dinner item.String hoppers are a popular dish in Tamil Nadu, Kerala, and Sri Lanka, often served during festive occasions or family gatherings. They are a staple in both vegetarian and non-vegetarian cuisines in these regions. Here's an overview of string hoppers:


Ingredients:

  • Rice Flour: The primary ingredient, which is mixed with hot water to form a dough.
  • Water: Used to knead the rice flour into a smooth, pliable dough.
  • Salt: Added for flavor.

Preparation:

Step 1:
Dough Preparation: Rice flour is mixed with hot water and salt to make a soft, smooth dough.

Step 2:
Pressing the Dough: The dough is then placed in a string hopper press (similar to a noodle or pasta maker) and pressed into thin, noodle-like strands.

Step 3:
Steaming: These strands are arranged in circular nests on small woven mats or trays, then steamed until cooked.

Serving:

String hoppers are typically served with a variety of side dishes, including:
  • Coconut Sambol: A spicy coconut relish.
  • Kiri Hodi: A mild, creamy coconut milk curry.
  • Dal Curry: A lentil-based dish.
  • Meat or Fish Curry: For non-vegetarian options.
