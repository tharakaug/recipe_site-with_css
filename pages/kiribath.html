Kiribath (Milk Rice)

A traditional Sri Lankan dish of creamy coconut milk rice, served on special occasions and celebrations

Kiribath, also known as Milk Rice, is a traditional Sri Lankan dish made by cooking rice with coconut milk. It's a dish of deep cultural significance, often prepared for special occasions, ceremonies, and festive events. The name "kiribath" comes from the Sinhala words "kiri" (meaning milk) and "bath" (meaning rice). Kiribath is not just a dish but a symbol of tradition and culture in Sri Lanka, reflecting the importance of rice and coconut in the country's culinary heritage.

Ingredients:

  • Rice: Usually white rice or a specific short-grain rice variety is used
  • Coconut milk: Freshly extracted or canned coconut milk, which gives the dish its rich, creamy texture
  • Salt: A pinch of salt is added to balance the sweetness of the coconut milk

Preparation:

1 Cooking the Rice: The rice is washed and boiled until it is nearly cooked.

2 Adding Coconut Milk: Once the rice is almost done, coconut milk is added along with a pinch of salt. The mixture is cooked on low heat until the rice absorbs the coconut milk.

3 Shaping and Serving: The cooked rice is spread on a flat surface, usually a tray, and flattened with the back of a spoon. It is then cut into diamond or square shapes before serving.

Serving:

Milk rice is typically served with various accompaniments like lunu miris (a spicy onion sambol), jaggery (a type of palm sugar), or even bananas. It can be eaten as a breakfast dish or as part of a larger meal.
