Pittu

Pittu is a beloved traditional Sri Lankan dish made from steamed cylinders of ground rice layered with freshly grated coconut. It's a versatile dish that can be enjoyed at any time of the day, often served with a variety of accompaniments that make it suitable for breakfast, lunch, or dinner. Pittu is cherished for its simplicity, versatility, and ability to pair with both sweet and savory dishes. Ingredients:

  • Rice Flour: The base ingredient, often roasted before mixing with water to form a crumbly dough.
  • Grated Coconut: Layered within the rice flour mixture, adding moisture and a subtle sweetness.
  • Water: Mixed with the rice flour to create the right texture for steaming.
  • Salt: To taste, usually added to the rice flour mixture.

Preparation:

Step 1:
Mixing the Dough: The rice flour is combined with water and salt to create a crumbly dough. The mixture should not be too wet or too dry.

Step 2:
Layering: The dough is then layered with grated coconut in a cylindrical mold called a "Pittu Bambuwa" or a perforated metal container.

Step 3:
Steaming: The filled mold is placed over a pot of boiling water and steamed until the pittu is cooked through. The steam cooks the rice flour, and the coconut infuses its flavor throughout the dish.

Serving:

Pittu is traditionally served with:
  • Coconut Milk: A thin or thick coconut milk gravy that enhances the dish's flavor.
  • Lunu Miris: A spicy sambal made from chili, onion, and lime, providing a hot and tangy contrast to the mild pittu.
  • Curries: Pittu pairs well with a variety of curries, such as chicken curry, mutton curry, or dhal (lentil) curry.
  • Sweet Options: Pittu can also be served with sugar or jaggery for a sweet treat, making it a versatile dish.
